Voluntary work at the Porcelain Tower of Nanjing

Voluntary Work at the Porcelain Tower of Nanjing\

On October 5th,me and other 3 students from PAL2 went to the Porcelain Tower of Nanjing for the voluntary work. I was chosen to be the team leader. As a leader, I set up a group in QQ which inclueded all our members and posted a range of announcements in it before the activity to make sure the work would be done well.

It was the very first time for me to be a real volunteer. Before that, I always wondered it could be like to do a voluntary work as I did care about helping the society. We first gathered at the entrance at 8:40a.m. Then, with the excitement and a little bit tension, we Started our journey as volunteers.

After signing in, we got our work from the manager in the volunteer center. That was the time we knew that we needed to work separately in 2 groups, two in each group. One group in needed in the Millennium underground palace. Another one was set in the depositarry of Buddhist texts. I worked in the Millennium underground palace. Then, we put on our yellow work clothes and hurried to our work location.

I worked together with Ye Liu in the palace. The mission we took was to make sure every tourist walked clockwise around the palace according to the Buddhist culture. The work lasted for about 8 hours. So it might be tiring for us .That’s why we decided to take turns to work. Each of us two worked for 1h each turn. Then we exchanged.

At first, we all got lost in the mysterious atmosphere area where Buddhist relics were placed. Meanwhile there wasn’t many tourists in the early morning. So the job seemed not that difficult for us. However, much more tourists started to get into the palace. Only few of them are Buddhist so most of the tourists didn’t realize they should go clockwise around. So we had to stand there still and remind people of the way they should go in a very polite and clear way. After 8 hours’ working, we all got exhausted. But we were also truly happy to see how well-ordered it was due to our voluntary work.

That was nearly the most meaningful thing I had ever done for the public. That made me think of how important and difficult it was to make the tourist attractions in good orders. So I will sure to follow the public rules and remind others of this ever since then. And many hands did make light work.
