Dance performance

Life is all about doing what you truly enjoy without worrying too much about success or failure.

Honestly, I used to be a very shy girl before entering senior high school. Every time I wanted to

attempt something, I always abandoned it at the very beginning because of my bashfulness.

However, a chance experience totally changed that.

Our school held an art festival during my time in senior 1 and I got the chance to join the dance group. I struggled a lot before making the decision as to whether I should join. I worried excessively about the consequences of not performing very well and being humiliated. Nonetheless, I finally decided to give it a try.

Then, the rehearsals began. I can still recall clearly that my legs wouldn’t stop shaking when I entered the studio. All the dance moves were linked closely together, so I found it hard to keep up with the dance moves and it was even tougher than I had imagined before. What’s worse, I had a terrible memory of the sequence of moves. When I went home, I found a few bruises on my knees and my legs. However, I totally ignored these and kept practicing hard because I was passionate about dancing. When the final performance came, I was placed on the back line in the group, and we didn’t get the first prize. Still, I enjoyed it a lot and considered this as an opportunity to dance for my own satisfaction.

It is clear that we are not able to succeed at everything we try to do. So, the most helpful attitude to have is to not push yourself too hard and to consider every challenge as an experience to promote your self-improvement. At least then you know you will not have any regrets because you have already shown yourself that you have the courage and ability to try.
