National Economics Challenge

  • National Economics Challenge

  • Economies

- Preparing

• I started to prepare for the competition in the November of the year 2020, about a month and a half before the competition. That was my first time to take part in such a formal Economies competition and I didn’t know the most efficient way to prepare . What’s more, we just learnt only a few topics at school at that time.So most of the knowledge were brand new for me. In order to catch up! with the high level of the competition, I tried to learn as much as possible in a few methods .

• Firstly, I made my own plan to read the books Principles of Economics . There are two books in total which contains two different topics, microeconomic and macroeconomic . Because of the limitation of time , I just chose several contents that I was not familiar with to read, starting from the microeconomic. I read as much as could during my spare time and took down notes to summarise the main points for me to review later.

• This was an example of the notes I took.

• Method 2 : Fortunately, there happened to be a great chance for me to take part in the NEC training class held in our school every Tuesday afternoon. The teacher took us to go over the following knowledges and topics in the Economies course-book and in the past papers. The lesson went so fast that I couldn’t keep up in time in class. So I had to take down some notes and record everything the teacher said ,then reviewed by myself after the lesson. The course lasted until a week before the final competition. Although this method was a little bit tiring and not that suitable and efficient for me, it was still undeniable that it definitely forced me to go over the most main points which was impossible to be done all by myself

• Method 3: As the second method was a little bit harsh for me, I came up with the third way at the same period of time: watching the videos one the official website and recorded by other teachers. That was almost the best way of all for me to learn new knowledges. Because it was convenient for me to stop the video at any part I wanted so that it was easier to understand the points and to take better notes.

• Apart from these methods , I also participated in the discussions about the questions with my classmates and team members a lot and I was willing to ask my teacher for help whenever I found the confusing knowledges.

- Final competition

• First came the simulated exam. We needed to treat it as seriously as the formal competition and it could help us get familiar with the competition process. I tried to found out the reasons I lost marks in it and reviews the points I needed to consolidate . However, I found the final competition very difficult for me and I didn’t get a satisfactory result, neither did my team. Although I tried my best , I still felt very sorry for other team members. However at any rate, we wouldn’t give up and would still be passionate about Economies.
