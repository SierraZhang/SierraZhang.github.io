Application paragraph

Application paragraph

While growing up, I was always proud of my ability to self-study without any help and I believed that this was the most efficient way of gaining knowledge.

In the mock exam during the second semester, I got a low mark in chemistry which frustrated me. Fortunately, this event forced me to review my previous learning method and set the goal of improving my mark. Firstly, I went over the answer sheet from the mock examination and filled out a table, designed by my teacher, to show the specific topics I lost the most marks on as well as the reasons I got answers wrong. Using the table as a guide, I did a lot of reading on the topics I was weak in. Then I asked a senior for help when it came to practicing. She gave me the past paper questions for this topic which was a helpful resource for me. After finishing the questions, I made a portfolio of all the questions I lost marks in. Later on, I found that there were still several complex topics that hadn’t been taught by my teacher. I discussed these topics with my friends at school. Some of them were better at chemistry and helped me to solve some confusing problems. When it came to parts that none of us could work out, we looked for video resources and made notes from the videos. I still found the naming principles for organic substances tricky, so I turned to my school teacher for help.

As a result of these new efforts, I found that my knowledge of chemistry significantly improved and I started to pay more attention to details which were skipped by me before as I did not have enough awareness through independent study. I am not saying that self-study is not an effective method. In my opinion, the most efficient way to learn is to ask for an appropriate amount of help based on your personal thinking and understanding of the study topics. Other people may have more experience than you and may be able to offer better suggestions to assist you in your study.
